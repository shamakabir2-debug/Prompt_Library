# 08 · Customer Segmentation and Churn Rate Analysis

## Prompt Text

> Act as a customer analytics specialist for the ShopGO e-commerce platform. Analyse the following customer data to segment users into meaningful groups and identify their churn risk.
>
> Provide:
> 1. Customer segment (e.g., high-value, frequent buyer, occasional shopper, at-risk customer)  
> 2. Churn risk level (Low / Medium / High)  
> 3. Key reasons for classification (e.g., reduced purchase frequency, declining engagement, low spending)  
>
> If the data is insufficient or inconsistent, classify the result as **"Review Required"**.
>
> **Customer data:**  
> Purchase history: [Insert transaction data]  
> Browsing behaviour: [Insert activity data]  
> Engagement metrics: [Insert frequency, recency, spending]

## Intended Workflow or Task

This prompt is used to support customer segmentation, churn prediction, and targeted retention strategies on the ShopGO platform.

## Problem Being Solved

E-commerce platforms often struggle to identify which customers are likely to stop purchasing and which are high-value users. Without proper segmentation, businesses cannot effectively target marketing efforts, leading to lost revenue, inefficient promotions, and poor customer retention.

## Automation Potential

**High.**  
Segmentation and churn analysis can be automated using customer data at scale. AI can continuously analyse large datasets and generate insights in real time, enabling proactive retention strategies. Human input is required for designing marketing campaigns and interpreting strategic implications.

## Risks and Limitations

- Use of customer behavioural data must comply with data protection and privacy regulations  
- Over-generalising segments may oversimplify complex customer behaviour  
- Incomplete or outdated data may affect segmentation accuracy  
- AI may not fully capture qualitative factors influencing customer behaviour
